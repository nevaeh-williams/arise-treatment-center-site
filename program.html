What We Do

A full continuum of care — from detox to daily life.

A CARF-accredited ASAM Level 3.5 residential program built around 28 to 30 days of structured living, clinical care, and 12-step recovery work — followed by transition into community-based care.

Program Overview

Structured living. Clinical care. 12-Step foundation.

ARISE Treatment Center is a CARF-accredited ASAM Level 3.5 residential licensed adult male facility. We help men gain the knowledge and strength to work the 12-steps of recovery, with progression and regression reported on a weekly basis.

Clients enter a 28 to 30 day residential program with structured living and programming 7 days a week, receiving case management, clinical group therapy, and individual therapy — all inside a safe, clean, drug-free environment.

Your Path

The journey through our program.

From the first phone call to transitional community living, here’s how care unfolds at ATC.

1

Intake & Assessment

Initial evaluation, physical and mental assessments, and a plan tailored to your history, strengths, and goals.

2

Detox with Residential

Medical withdrawal management with comfort medications, under the care of our doctor, nurse practitioner, and RN/LPN team.

3

Residential Treatment

28–30 days of structured living, clinical group, individual therapy, case management, and 12-step work.

4

Transition & IOP

Referral to Intensive Outpatient Program or lower level of care based on your preference, with continued sponsor and 12-step support.

How We Can Help

Four levels of care, one continuous path.

Holistic, Person-Centered Treatment

For adults dealing with poly-substance abuse disorder who may also experience chronic or urgent medical and mental health issues. Non-judgmental, non-biased, trauma-informed care.

Detox with Residential

Medical withdrawal management and comfort medications, followed by 28–30 days of residential care. Dual-diagnosis treatment addresses underlying mental health conditions.

Community Connections

Step-down to the transitional level of care with continued 24-hour support and reintegration through 12-step meetings — particularly beneficial for dual-diagnosis clients.

Transition Support

Clinical group sessions five days a week, peer support, and case management through the Residential Transitional phase — approximately four weeks.

What’s Inside

Your treatment team and your curriculum.

The Treatment Team

Credentialed clinicians at every step.

  • Doctor & Nurse Practitioner
  • RN / LPN nursing staff
  • Licensed counselors and therapists
  • Program Manager and case managers

Individual therapy is provided by licensed and credentialed clinical staff including LCSWs, LMHCs, LCACs, LMFTs, LSWs, and MHCs (under supervision of a fully licensed LCSW).

Curriculum Topics

Evidence-based, CBT-derived.

• Addiction & the brain • Anger & shame • Alcohol & drug education • Denial • Trauma & addiction • Emotional regulation • Family dynamics • Communication • Forgiveness • Life management

Individual therapy also covers relapse prevention, triggers and coping skills, medication compliance, and other issues clients wish to address.
